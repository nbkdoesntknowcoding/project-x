# BOPPL — Onboarding voice and tone

When you write to a new prospect for the first time, optimise for one thing: making it easy to say "yes, tell me more." Everything else is downstream of that.

## What this means in practice

- **Open with the problem they have, not the product we have.** "I noticed your team ships weekly to a 50-person Slack" lands better than "We built a tool that…"
- **Keep paragraphs short.** Three sentences max. The reader is on a phone between meetings.
- **End with a single question, not three.** Multiple asks dilute the response rate.

## What good looks like

> Hey Priya, saw the new pricing change on QuickQuote. The shift to per-seat is going to surface a lot of "but my team only uses it twice a week" pushback in renewals — we've helped two B2B SaaS teams set up usage-based fallbacks for exactly that case. Worth 15 minutes next week?

That's 60 words, one specific observation, one specific offer, one question.

## What to avoid

- Walls of text
- Vague claims ("we help teams scale")
- Multiple CTAs
- Anything that sounds like a template
